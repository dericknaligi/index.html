The Problem Statement

In Nairobi, GBV remains a critical barrier to women's empowerment.

Approximately 47% of women aged 15-49 experience physical or sexual violence in their lifetime

           (Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, 2020).

 This pervasive issue causes severe physical and psychological harm,

perpetuating cycles of poverty and inequality that limit women's access to education,

economic opportunities, and participation in decision-making processes ( UN Women, 2021)

Despite existing legal frameworks and initiatives aimed at addressing GBV,


societal stigma, cultural norms, and inadequate support systems prevent many survivors

from seeking help and reclaiming their agency (Human Rights Watch, 2020).

Addressing GBV is crucial for creating a safer and more equitable society,

enabling women to thrive and contribute meaningfully to their communities

(National Council on Gender and Development, 2021).
